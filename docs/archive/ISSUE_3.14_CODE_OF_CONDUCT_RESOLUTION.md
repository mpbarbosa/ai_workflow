## Code of Conduct Structure

### 1. Our Pledge

Commitment to harassment-free participation for everyone, regardless of:
- Age, body size, disability
- Ethnicity, gender identity
- Experience level, education
- Nationality, race, religion
- Sexual identity and orientation

### 2. Our Standards

**Positive Behaviors**:
- ‚úÖ Demonstrating empathy and kindness
- ‚úÖ Respecting differing opinions
- ‚úÖ Giving and accepting constructive feedback
- ‚úÖ Accepting responsibility for mistakes
- ‚úÖ Focusing on community benefit

**Unacceptable Behaviors**:
- ‚ùå Sexualized language or advances
- ‚ùå Trolling, insults, personal attacks
- ‚ùå Public or private harassment
- ‚ùå Publishing private information
- ‚ùå Inappropriate professional conduct

### 3. Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers will:
- Clarify and enforce standards
- Take appropriate corrective action
- Remove/edit inappropriate contributions
- Communicate moderation decisions

### 4. Scope

Applies to:
- All community spaces
- Official representation in public
- Online and offline events
- Social media with official accounts

### 5. Enforcement

**Reporting**: Via GitHub issues or direct contact  
**Response**: Prompt and fair investigation  
**Privacy**: Respect for reporter confidentiality

### 6. Enforcement Guidelines (4 Levels)

#### Level 1: Correction
- **Impact**: Inappropriate language, unprofessional behavior
- **Consequence**: Private warning, explanation, possible public apology

#### Level 2: Warning
- **Impact**: Single incident or series of violations
- **Consequence**: Warning with no-interaction period, possible ban

#### Level 3: Temporary Ban
- **Impact**: Serious violation, sustained inappropriate behavior
- **Consequence**: Temporary ban from all community interaction

#### Level 4: Permanent Ban
- **Impact**: Pattern of violations, harassment, aggression
- **Consequence**: Permanent ban from all community interaction

## Enforcement in Practice

### Reporting Process

1. **Report Received**
   - Via issue, contact, or community report
   - Acknowledged within 24 hours
   - Reporter privacy maintained

2. **Investigation**
   - Review reported behavior
   - Gather context and evidence
   - Consult with other maintainers if needed

3. **Decision**
   - Determine if violation occurred
   - Select appropriate enforcement level
   - Document decision

4. **Action**
   - Communicate with involved parties
   - Apply consequence
   - Monitor compliance

5. **Follow-Up**
   - Check on reporter
   - Monitor situation
   - Adjust if needed

### Enforcement Examples

**Level 1 - Correction**:
- First-time minor language issue
- Unintentional offensive comment
- Response: Private warning, explanation

**Level 2 - Warning**:
- Repeated inappropriate comments
- Ignoring first warning
- Response: Public warning, interaction limits

**Level 3 - Temporary Ban**:
- Sustained harassment
- Multiple warnings ignored
- Response: 30-day ban, no interaction

**Level 4 - Permanent Ban**:
- Pattern of harassment
- Doxxing, threats
- Response: Permanent removal

### Q: Why Contributor Covenant?

**A**: Industry standard used by 100,000+ projects including Linux, Rails, Angular. Proven effective and legally sound.

### Q: How do I report a violation?

**A**: Open a confidential issue mentioning @mpbarbosa, contact directly via GitHub, or use GitHub's community reporting.

### Q: What if I disagree with a decision?

**A**: Contact project maintainers with additional context. Decisions can be reviewed and adjusted.

### Q: Does this apply to private messages?

**A**: Yes, if representing the project. Also applies to harassment via private channels.

### Q: Who enforces the Code of Conduct?

**A**: Project maintainers, primarily @mpbarbosa. All maintainers follow the 4-level enforcement guidelines.

### Q: Is this legally binding?

**A**: It's a community standard, not a legal contract. However, violations can lead to removal from the project.
